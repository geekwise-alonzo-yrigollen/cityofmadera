Council Member Donald E. Holley Madera City Council District 6 District 6 Map

Donald Holley is currently serving his first term in office as a Madera City Council Member. Donald was first elected to the City Council in 2012 for a four year term through 2016.

Donald served as Mayor Pro Tem in 2013. The Mayor Pro Tem position is rotated annually amongst the Council Members serving.

Members of the City Council also serve as Board Members of the Successor Agency to the Former Madera Redevelopment Agency and as Commissioners of the Housing Authority of the City of Madera.

Donald was born and raised in Madera. He attended Madera schools and graduated from Madera High School in 1969. After high school he attended Fresno City College and completed his studies in 1971.

Donald is well known in the community; most notably for his commitment to the McNally Park neighborhood and his leadership role in the McNally Park Jesse Owens Games since 1988, a track and field event for youth. He has also served on the Madera County Planning Commission, as president of the local branch of the NAACP, and on the MLK Host Committee. Donald has received numerous awards and recognitions for his service to the community.

Donald is married to Joan Holley. They have seven children Reginald, Annette, Roxanne, Rodney, Anthony, Leona, and Donald II. They also have 15 grandchildren and 3 great-grandchildren.

In addition to their regular duties as members of the City Council, Members of the City Council are appointed to serve on other boards as representatives of the City Council. They are also active in community organizations.
